One of the most exciting developments in the AI world recently is the rise of **agentic workflows**. Unlike traditional chatbots, AI agents are systems that can make autonomous decisions, use tools, and complete multi-step tasks to achieve specific goals.

## What is Agentic AI?

Agentic AI is an approach that combines large language models (LLMs) with tools, memory, and planning capabilities to create autonomous systems capable of solving real-world problems.

### Core Components

- **Planning:** Breaking complex tasks into subtasks
- **Tool Use:** Interacting with APIs, databases, and external services
- **Memory:** Short and long-term context management
- **ReAct (Reason + Act):** Cyclical reasoning loops

## Frameworks I Use

### LangChain

LangChain is one of the most popular frameworks for LLM applications. It offers rapid prototyping with chains, agents, and memory modules.

### LangGraph

LangGraph is a library designed for stateful and cyclic agent workflows. I used this framework in the Hybrid Agent Platform project during my internship at The Leapers.

### LlamaIndex

LlamaIndex provides a powerful data connection and indexing solution, especially for RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) scenarios.
